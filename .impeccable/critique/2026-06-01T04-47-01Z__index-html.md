## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment:** Does not scream "AI made this," but a discerning eye sees the scaffolding. The foundation is genuinely tasteful: a committed warm-editorial palette, a real Syne + DM Sans pairing, a real photograph, confident headlines. The tells are compositional: (1) a tiny uppercase tracked eyebrow + 36px underline sits above *every* section, centered, with identical rhythm — the saturated 2023 kicker scaffold; (2) the four-stat row (25+ / 7 / 242+ / 8) sits near the SaaS hero-metric template; (3) every section header is centered with the same cadence, so the page has one uniform vertical rhythm rather than art-directed variation.

**Deterministic scan:** `detect.mjs` returned 1 finding — `single-font` ("only DM Sans used"). **False positive:** the detector reads markup only and cannot see that headings use Syne via `--font-heading` in `style.css`, with both families loaded in `<head>`. The page runs a real two-font system. No true anti-patterns flagged by the scanner.

**Visual overlays:** Not available. The MCP browser is pinned to an uninstalled Chrome stable channel, so script injection / the in-page overlay could not run. Visual review was done from direct Chromium screenshots at 1280 / 768 / 390 instead; no user-visible overlay exists in a browser tab.

## Overall Impression

A credible, warm, well-built single-page resume site that mostly succeeds at "earned authority." It reads as a real person with a real career, which is the whole job. The biggest opportunity is compositional confidence: the section scaffolding (eyebrow + underline + centered title, every time) and the stat row are the parts most likely to read as generic. Tighten those and the page moves from "nicely templated" to "art-directed." The one functional risk is body-text contrast sitting at the AA floor.

## What's Working

- **Committed, on-register palette.** Warm cream paper with a single terracotta accent and gold grace note is distinctive and consistent; it carries the "warm, editorial, credible" brief without resorting to a stock-photo corporate look.
- **The experience section.** Prose role cards with a gold date eyebrow, Syne role headline, terracotta company, and a full-height gradient spine that widens on hover are the most art-directed part of the page, and the current-role gold wash is a smart, subtle signal.
- **Responsive discipline.** No headline overflow at 768 or 390; hamburger appears correctly; hero buttons stay in the mobile thumb zone; photo and headline reflow cleanly.

## Priority Issues

- **[P1] Eyebrow-on-every-section scaffolding.** Summary / Career / Education / Expertise / Press / Contact each carry the same tiny uppercase tracked label + underline, centered. **Why it matters:** This is the single most recognizable AI-grammar tell on the page; repeated identically it reads as scaffold, not voice, and undercuts the "not AI slop" goal in PRODUCT.md. **Fix:** Keep the eyebrow on at most one or two sections (or retire it), and vary section-header composition (left-align some, drop the underline, let a few sections open straight on the headline). **Suggested command:** `/impeccable typeset`
- **[P1] Body text at the contrast floor.** `--color-text-secondary` (#78716C) on cream (#FAF7F2) computes to ~4.49:1 and dips below 4.5:1 on the `--color-bg-alt` sand sections, yet it carries the summary and experience prose. **Why it matters:** Fails WCAG AA for normal text on the alt sections; this is the "muted gray on tinted near-white" readability trap. **Fix:** Use `--color-text` (#292524) for long body copy, or darken the secondary token until it clears 4.5:1 on sand. **Suggested command:** `/impeccable audit`
- **[P2] Stat row reads as the hero-metric template.** The four big-number/small-label cards (25+ / 7 / 242+ / 8) are close to the SaaS cliché. **Why it matters:** It is the second-most templated element after the eyebrows, and "242+ Projects Delivered" invites scrutiny on a resume. **Fix:** Recast as a short inline prose line, reduce to the two numbers that genuinely matter, or restyle away from the metric-card grid. **Suggested command:** `/impeccable distill`
- **[P2] Uniform centered rhythm.** Every section uses the same centered header + generous symmetric spacing, so the page has no compositional peaks. **Why it matters:** Brand surfaces earn memorability through art-directed variation; uniformity is the safe-equals-invisible failure. **Fix:** Vary alignment and density per section; let the experience and work sections breathe differently from the summary. **Suggested command:** `/impeccable layout`
- **[P2] Experience card padding cramps the measure on mobile.** Cards keep `2rem 3rem` padding at 390px (no small-screen override), squeezing prose into a narrow column inside the already-padded container. **Why it matters:** Short, choppy line lengths hurt readability of the most important content. **Fix:** Reduce horizontal card padding at the 480/768 breakpoints. **Suggested command:** `/impeccable adapt`

## Persona Red Flags

**Sam (Accessibility-Dependent):** Body copy in `--color-text-secondary` fails AA on the sand sections (~4.3–4.49:1). Need to confirm visible focus indicators on nav links and form fields and meaningful alt text on portfolio images. Color-only cues (terracotta = link/active) should have a non-color affordance.

**Casey (Distracted Mobile):** Hero and buttons are reachable, but the contact form sits at the very bottom of a long single-page scroll with no sticky "contact" affordance once you're deep in the page (the nav CTA scrolls away until the bar re-pins). Experience prose runs narrow on mobile due to card padding.

**Pat (Hiring Manager / Recruiter — project persona):** Skimming fast to judge credibility and grab the resume. The stat "242+ Projects Delivered" reads as inflated on a resume and could erode trust. There is no obvious "download / print resume" action surfaced, despite the repositioning intent; a recruiter who wants the PDF has to improvise.

## Minor Observations

- Nav carries 7 links + a CTA (8 targets), above the ≤5 working-memory guideline; consider grouping (e.g. fold Press into Work, or Education into Summary).
- The contact form still exists, though the approved repositioning spec called for dropping it in favor of LinkedIn/GitHub links. Worth reconciling intent vs. implementation.
- `text-wrap: balance` on headings and `pretty` on long prose would tighten line breaks (not currently used).

## Questions to Consider

- Does a resume site need a contact *form*, or does "email + LinkedIn + download resume" serve the recruiter faster?
- Is "242+ Projects Delivered" a number that helps or invites doubt?
- What would a confident, art-directed version of the Summary section look like if it broke the centered rhythm?
