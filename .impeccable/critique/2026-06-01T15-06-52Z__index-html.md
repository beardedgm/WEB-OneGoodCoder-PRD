## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**Does this look AI-generated? Mostly no — it reads as a tasteful designer's portfolio, but two card-grid sections give back ground the Experience section wins.**

**LLM assessment:** The **Experience "CV spread"** is the page's signature and is genuinely crafted: a two-column meta-rail + prose with hairline rules, no card chrome, the current role on a soft gold band with a "Current" pill. That is a real editorial decision, not a SaaS reflex, and it carries the "Editorial Résumé" north star. Palette discipline is honest (terracotta truly sparing, gold the quiet second, never pure white), and Syne + DM Sans is committed. The tells that remain: **Publications use a `border-left: 3px` side-stripe** (an explicit editorial-brand DON'T) across a 12-card grid; **Skills is a uniform 2-col feature-card grid**; **Education has a `linear-gradient` accent bar** (SaaS cliché); and **three section subtitles restate their headings**. The strongest sections (About, Skills, Education) have no subtitle and are better for it.

**Deterministic scan:** `detect.mjs` flagged 1 warning — `single-font` ("only DM Sans used"), at `index.html:1`. **False positive:** Syne is used for every display heading via `--font-heading`; the detector isn't resolving the CSS font assignment. No other antipatterns flagged — notably the detector did *not* catch the publications side-stripe or the education gradient, both of which Assessment A found by eye, so the manual review is doing the heavy lifting here.

**Visual overlays:** Browser visualization was run via the bundled Chromium with full-page screenshots at desktop (1280) and mobile (390); no user-visible overlay was injected (static-file render, not the live-server overlay flow). Console: **zero errors** across both viewports. Confirmed by computed style: `.hero__title` loads at `opacity: 0` (entrance-gated), and the flagged CSS exists at `style.css:988` (publications side-stripe) and `style.css:719` (education gradient).

## Overall Impression

This is a credible, warm, well-built résumé site that a skimming recruiter would respect. The Experience redesign is the high point and proves the brand can do editorial restraint. The single biggest opportunity: **carry that same typographic-list discipline into Publications and Skills** (which currently fall back to the card grids the brand warns against), and **re-point the closing CTA at the action a recruiter would actually take** (connect/email) instead of "Print." Fixing those would move the page from "tasteful template" to "clearly crafted by this person."

## What's Working

1. **The Experience CV spread.** Meta-rail + prose, hairline dividers, gold "Current" band — the one section most résumé sites botch into another card grid, solved with taste. It alone justifies the design system.
2. **Modal accessibility is properly engineered.** `role="dialog"`, `aria-modal`, focus moves to close on open and returns to the triggering card on close, Escape + backdrop + button all close, body scroll locked. Better than most production sites.
3. **Print and reduced-motion are real, not afterthoughts.** `@media print` hides nav/decoration, forces `.reveal` visible (no blank printed sections), flips the dark footer light, and avoids page-breaks inside roles. Reduced-motion restores all hero/reveal content to `opacity: 1`. Content is never permanently gated behind a transition.

## Priority Issues

**[P1] Portfolio titles are hidden until hover — invisible on touch.**
- **Why it matters:** `.portfolio-card__overlay { opacity: 0 }` reveals the title/tech only on `:hover`. On a phone (no hover) the titles *never* appear — a recruiter taps six dark, desaturated mystery thumbnails to learn what each is. This is the core of "Selected Work" failing for the exact persona (mobile recruiter) it's for. The default `saturate(0.85) brightness(0.96)` makes already-dark church screenshots murkier.
- **Fix:** Show a persistent title/caption on every card (a bottom label bar); reserve the overlay/zoom for hover *enhancement*. Add a `(hover: none)` rule that shows the overlay statically. Lighten the default image filter.
- **Suggested command:** `/impeccable adapt` (touch/hover behavior), then `/impeccable layout`.

**[P1] The closing CTA leads with the wrong action, and there's no email.**
- **Why it matters:** In the Contact block "Print résumé" is the dominant terracotta primary; "Connect on LinkedIn" is the secondary outline. The page's whole goal is "recruiter decides to reach out" — printing is a low-intent clerical task, useless on the phone where many will view this, and it's visually winning the conversion moment. No `mailto:` exists at all, so the most direct channel is missing.
- **Fix:** Make "Connect on LinkedIn" (and/or a `mailto:`) the primary; demote Print to outline or a small text link. Add a direct email contact.
- **Suggested command:** `/impeccable clarify` (CTA hierarchy + labels), supported by `/impeccable layout`.

**[P2] Publications and Skills fall back to the card grids the brand forbids.**
- **Why it matters:** Publications are 12 near-identical white cards each carrying a `border-left: 3px` gold side-stripe (`style.css:988`) — a named editorial-brand DON'T — and the wall of sameness sags the energy right before the closing CTA. Education's accent bar is a `linear-gradient(90deg, accent, gold)` (`style.css:719`), another cliché. These are the page's most templated moments and they undercut the credibility the Experience section builds.
- **Fix:** Replace the publication side-stripe with a typographic, scannable list (year · title · source) or a top hairline; collapse the 12 cards into a denser format. Swap the education gradient bar for a solid gold hairline.
- **Suggested command:** `/impeccable distill` (publications density) + `/impeccable layout`; `/impeccable quieter` for the gradient/stripe.

**[P2] Experience reads as responsibilities, not results — nothing to skim.**
- **Why it matters:** Every role is a uniform dense 5–6 line paragraph in the same register; they blur together. There are no bolded outcomes or numbers for an 8-second skim, and the one real metric ("500K+ annual visitors") is buried inside a portfolio modal a mobile recruiter will never open. A leader's résumé that lists duties without outcomes reads as junior.
- **Fix:** Surface 1–2 outcome/metric highlights per recent role (a lead line, a bolded figure, or a short stat in the meta-rail). Pull the strongest numbers out of the modals.
- **Suggested command:** `/impeccable clarify` (content/copy hierarchy); `/impeccable layout` if adding a stat element.

**[P2] Nav label/heading mismatch, and the nav is heavy for a one-pager.**
- **Why it matters:** 7 links + a CTA = 8 targets (over the ≤4–5 skim guideline), and clicking "Press" or "Work" lands on a heading that doesn't echo the word, a small consistency tax every time.
- **Fix:** Align nav wording with section headings (or add the nav word as the section eyebrow); consider trimming to the 4–5 destinations a recruiter actually jumps to.
- **Suggested command:** `/impeccable clarify`.

## Persona Red Flags

**Pat — recruiter skimming on a phone between meetings (project persona):**
- **Selected Work is unusable for her:** six unlabeled dark thumbnails, titles never appear without hover. She can't tell Woodlands Church from Evolve Money without tapping each.
- **Nothing to skim:** uniform dense paragraphs, no bolded outcomes; the one metric is hidden in a modal she won't open on mobile.
- **At the decision moment the big button says "Print résumé"** — useless on a phone; the action she'd take (LinkedIn/email) is demoted, and there's no email at all.

**Casey (mobile):** Portfolio hover-titles fail. Tap targets are otherwise well handled (`pointer: coarse` → 44px buttons, drawer rows, modal close), but the inline connect links (`linkedin.com/in/...`, `github.com/beardedgm`) are small `--font-size-sm` text in a wrapping row, fiddly to tap.

**Sam (a11y):** `--color-text-secondary` (#645D57) is used for long body copy (about, experience summaries, modal text). It *passes* AA on the darkest background it lands on (≈5.45:1 on `--color-bg-alt` #F0EBE3, ≈6.0:1 on the gold band), so this is acceptable, but the brand guidance prefers `--color-text` for long prose. The skip link targets `#about`, jumping past the hero (name/title) — point it at `#top` or a `<main>` landmark.

## Minor Observations

- Hero content is entrance-gated: `.hero__title` etc. start at `opacity: 0` and fade in over ~1.45s (last delay 0.65s + 0.8s). Reduced-motion restores them to `opacity: 1`, so it's safe there, but for motion users on a slow load the first ~1.5s is a near-blank cream hero — and it technically violates the brand's own "never gate content behind a reveal" rule. Make the hero visible by default and let the motion be additive.
- `detect.mjs` `single-font` warning is a false positive (Syne is in active use).
- Two experience entries overlap in time (HCN 1998–2001, Vicksburg Post 1998–2000); a sharp recruiter may notice.
- `og:image` / JSON-LD reference `images/Imug.jpg` — a sloppy filename for a details-oriented brand.
- Favicon is the 💻 emoji; a monogram would be more on-brand.
- Console: zero errors. Reduced-motion and print paths verified clean.

## Questions to Consider

1. The Experience section proves you can do editorial restraint — so why do Publications and Skills fall back to the exact card grids the brand says to avoid? What if the whole page committed to the CV-spread / typographic-list logic and kept cards only for the single Education credential?
2. This is a résumé for a leader, but it reads like a list of responsibilities. Where are the results? If "500K+ annual visitors" exists, why is it buried in a modal instead of the loudest thing in the Experience rail?
3. At the moment a recruiter decides to act, the loudest button is "Print." Who prints a résumé in 2026, and is that the right thing to optimize the conversion moment for over "connect" / "email me"?
4. Is repeating "Best Church Websites 20XX" twelve times more convincing than one tight line ("Recognized 12× across 2016–2022 by …"), or does the volume start to read as padding?
