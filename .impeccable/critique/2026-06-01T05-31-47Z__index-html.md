## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment: now clean.** The compositional tells that flagged last run are gone: no eyebrow kicker on every section, no four-card hero-metric row, no left-edge accent spine. What remains reads as a deliberate warm-editorial document, carried by the Syne/DM Sans pairing, the single terracotta accent, and the gold grace note. The biggest remaining "templated" quality is that all section headers are still centered with the same cadence, which is a deliberate choice the owner kept (the spacing rhythm now varies bare-title vs subtitled sections), not a reflex.

**Deterministic scan:** `detect.mjs` returned 1 finding — `single-font`. **Still a false positive:** markup-only scan can't see Syne applied via `--font-heading` in `style.css`. No true anti-patterns.

**Visual overlays:** Not available (MCP browser pinned to an uninstalled Chrome channel). Review done from direct Chromium screenshots at 1280 / 390 with touch emulation; no in-page overlay exists.

## Overall Impression

The page moved from "tasteful but visibly templated" to "clean and intentional." The headline change since last run is the Anti-Patterns verdict flipping to clean, plus WCAG AA contrast now passing across the warm palette (a real accessibility win the Nielsen total doesn't fully reflect). The Nielsen score only nudges 31→32 because the usability heuristics were never the problem; the remaining drag is entirely the contact area (error prevention/recovery at 2 each) and the missing resume accelerator. That is where the next points live.

## What's Working

- **The AI-slop tells are resolved.** Eyebrows, stat template, and side-stripe are gone; the page no longer reads as machine-templated, which was the top brand risk in PRODUCT.md.
- **Accessibility cleared.** Body, tertiary, and gold-eyebrow text all pass AA now (verified by computed contrast), and touch targets are 44px on coarse pointers. The structure was already strong (ARIA, labels, focus-visible, semantic headings).
- **System consistency.** Brand alphas are single-sourced through `--color-accent-rgb` / `--color-gold-rgb`, dead CSS/JS removed, spacing rhythm now varies by content.

## Priority Issues

- **[P1] The contact form is the weakest surface and conflicts with the repositioning intent.** Error prevention and recovery both score 2: minimal validation, generic error copy, no submit loading state. Meanwhile the approved repositioning spec called for dropping the form in favor of LinkedIn/GitHub links plus a resume action. **Why it matters:** it is the single biggest score drag and a documented intent mismatch. **Fix:** decide the flow, then either harden the form (inline validation, field-specific errors, loading + success states) or replace it per the spec. **Suggested command:** `/impeccable harden` (keep + strengthen) or `/impeccable craft` (replace with links + resume).
- **[P2] No resume download/print action.** A recruiter who wants the PDF has to improvise. **Why it matters:** it is the primary job-to-be-done for the audience (Pat the hiring manager). **Fix:** add a clear "Download résumé" / print action in the hero and/or contact area. **Suggested command:** `/impeccable craft`
- **[P3] Section rhythm is still uniformly centered.** Addressed only via spacing (per the owner's conservative choice); alignment is unchanged. **Why it matters:** minor; it is the last lingering "safe" quality. **Fix:** optional, revisit alignment variety if desired later. **Suggested command:** `/impeccable layout`

## Persona Red Flags

**Sam (Accessibility-Dependent):** Contrast now passes AA on all text including the sand sections; focus-visible outline and labels are present. Clear on the prior blockers. Remaining: the contact form lacks field-specific error association (`aria-describedby` on invalid fields).

**Pat (Hiring Manager / Recruiter):** Credibility reads well now ("242+ Projects" doubt is gone). Still no obvious way to grab the résumé as a file; the only conversion path is a contact form that may not match how a recruiter wants to engage.

**Casey (Distracted Mobile):** Tap targets are now 44px and card padding is comfortable. The contact form still sits at the bottom of a long scroll with no persistent contact affordance.

## Minor Observations

- Contact form fields could associate errors with `aria-describedby` for screen readers.
- Nav remains 7 links + CTA (kept deliberately as a one-page index).
- No submit loading state on the contact form (button doesn't indicate in-flight).

## Questions to Consider

- Should the résumé be downloadable/printable as a file, given that is what a recruiter most wants?
- Does a contact *form* still belong here, or do email + LinkedIn + a résumé download serve the audience better (as the repositioning spec suggested)?
- If the form stays, what is the smallest set of states that would make it feel trustworthy (inline validation, loading, success)?
